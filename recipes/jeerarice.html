Jeera Rice

This variation on plain basmati rice is mildly spiced, fragrant and tastes too good to be true.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tej patta
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1/2 inch cinnamon piece
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 to 2 strands of mace
  • 1 tsp salt or as required
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 green chilli - chopped
  • t bsp chopped coriander leaves

Recipe

  1. Rinse basmati rice till the water runs clear of starch. Soak the rice for 30 mins and then drain all the water. Keep aside.
  2. Heat 4 cups of water till it comes to a boil. Add all the whole spices. Add rice and salt. Simmer the rice on a low flame uncovered.
  3. The rice grains should be cooked till tender and yet separate. Drain the water and keep the cooked rice aside.
  4. Run some cool water over the rice to stop the cooking process immediately.
  5. Heat oil in a small pan. Add the cumin seeds.
  6. Once they start crakling, add the chopped green chillis. Don't over fry the chillis; retain that nice green colour.
  7. Add the tempering to the hot or warm rice.
  8. Very gently stir so that the tempering spreads evenly in the rice.
  9. Top the rice with the chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot.
